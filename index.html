What is Fear of Failure?

Fear of failure is when you are very worried about making mistakes or not achieving your goals. People with a fear of failure often feel stressed, nervous, or anxious when they have to complete tasks where they might be judged by others. This can happen at school, in sports, with friends, or in everyday situations. Many people with this fear worry that others will think less of them if they fail, which can stop them from trying new things or taking risks.

Can You Relate?

If you're between 15 and 18 years old in New Zealand, you may have experienced the fear of failure in different parts of your life. Whether you're at school, working, looking for a job, or figuring out what you want to do in the future, it's normal to feel pressure. You might worry about disappointing your family, making the wrong decisions, not meeting expectations, or comparing yourself to others on social media. For some teenagers, especially those from cultures where success is highly valued, these expectations can feel even greater. Remember, you're not alone. Many young people experience these feelings, and failure doesn't define your future, it helps you learn, grow, and become stronger.

image3

Overcoming Fear of Failure

Overcoming fear of failure takes time, but there are many ways to make it easier. Start by changing the way you think about failure and see it as an opportunity to learn rather than something to avoid. Set realistic goals and focus on making progress instead of being perfect. Challenge negative thoughts and remind yourself that everyone makes mistakes. Try new things, even if they feel uncomfortable, as this can help build confidence over time. It is also important to celebrate small achievements and ask for support from friends, family, or teachers when needed. By learning from mistakes and continuing to move forward, you can become more confident and less afraid of failure.
